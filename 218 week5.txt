“…scientific objectivity as conventionally understood is
predicated on the positionality of a disembodied master
subject with transcendent vision, which [Donna Haraway]
describes as ‘the god-trick of seeing everything from
nowhere’—where the knower is capable of achieving a
detached view into a separate, completely knowable
world… This kind of knowledge denies the partiality of
the knower, erases subjectivities, and ignores the nexus
of power-knowledge in its discursive practice. Feminist
critics see this mode of knowledge production as
masculinist.” –Kwan, p. 647
